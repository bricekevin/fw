M5Clawd

A standalone desk display for your Claude Code usage.

M5Clawd is a small WiFi device (an M5Stack Core, ESP32) that sits on your desk and shows your current 5-hour session and weekly Claude Code rate-limit usage — no host computer, no companion app once it is set up.

How setup works

M5Clawd never runs its own login. Instead you mint a token on your computer with Anthropic's official command and hand it to the device by scanning one QR code. Three steps:

  1. Power on the device. It creates its own WiFi network (M5Clawd-XXXXXX). Scan the on-screen QR to join it, then open the setup page and enter your home WiFi.
  2. Run the pairing helper on your Mac or PC (see below). It walks you through claude setup-token and shows your token as a QR code in your browser.
  3. Scan the helper's QR with your phone's camera while still on the device's setup network. That hands the token to M5Clawd — it restarts and starts showing your usage.

Get the pairing helper

The helper is a single Python script — macOS, Windows, or Linux. It needs Python 3 and a Claude subscription.

m5clawd-pair.py

python3 m5clawd-pair.py

It will offer to run claude setup-token for you, then serve the QR page at http://localhost:8765.

Why a helper, and not on-device login?

Generating the credential on a computer uses Anthropic's supported setup-token flow, and the long-lived token means the device rarely needs to talk to the login servers at all. The device stays simple and standalone; the computer is only needed once, at setup.
